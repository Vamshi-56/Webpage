What is an OTP Scam?

OTP (One-Time Password) scams are a type of fraud where criminals trick you into revealing the temporary codes sent to your phone or email. These codes are meant to verify your identity but scammers use them to gain access to your accounts.

Never share your OTP with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank, a government agency, or a trusted company.

How OTP Scams Work

1

The Initial Contact

Scammers contact you via phone call, SMS, email, or social media pretending to be from a legitimate organization.

2

The Urgent Request

They create a sense of urgency - your account is compromised, you'll lose money, or you need to verify something immediately.

3

The OTP Request

They ask you to share the OTP sent to your phone, often claiming it's needed to "verify your identity" or "stop a transaction."

4

The Theft

With your OTP, they gain access to your accounts, make unauthorized transactions, or steal personal information.

Common OTP Scam Examples

Fake Bank Calls

"This is your bank's security department. We've detected suspicious activity. Please share the OTP we just sent to verify it's you."

Delivery Scams

"Your package is held up. Share the OTP to confirm delivery address." (Even if you're not expecting a package)

Tech Support

"We're from Microsoft support. Your computer has a virus. Share the OTP to verify we're helping the right person."

Account Verification

"Your WhatsApp/Google/Facebook account will be deleted. Share the OTP to keep your account active."

Lottery/Winning Scams

"You've won a prize! Share the OTP to claim your winnings."

SIM Swap Scams

"We're upgrading our network. Share the OTP to keep your number active."

How to Protect Yourself

Never Share OTPs

Legitimate organizations will never ask for your OTP. Treat it like your password.

Verify Callers

Hang up and call back using official numbers from the company's website.

Don't Rush

Scammers create urgency. Take time to verify before acting.

Enable Alerts

Set up transaction alerts from your bank to monitor account activity.

Test Your Knowledge

Report OTP Scams

If you encounter an OTP scam attempt, report it to:

  • Your local police cybercrime unit
  • Your bank or financial institution
  • The relevant service provider (e.g., phone company, email provider)
  • National cybersecurity agencies in your country
